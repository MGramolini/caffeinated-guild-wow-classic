Blackwing Lair

Thanks for checking out our strategies for Blackwing Lair! BWL is a 40 man raid and has eight boss encounters. We will be clearing this as our main raid during phase 3 and 4 of WoW Classic!

First things first, getting attuned

  1. Kill the Scarshield Quartermaster inside Blackrock Mountain between the Orb of Command and Blackrock Spire.
  2. Loot the Blackhand's Command and accept the quest.
  3. Clear UBRS and interact with the Orb of Ascension where General Drakkisath is.

Boss Encounters

Razorgore the Untamed

Tanks: 4

The first boss fight has two phases; egg phase and burn phase. The egg phase is first and we will split the raid into four groups and send a team to each corner of the room.

Phase 1 (Egg)

We will most likely have 1-2 Paladins controlling Razorgore via the control orb to destroy the eggs in the room. While this is happening, the four teams will kill the adds that are spawning with priority Mage > Dragon > Legionaire. We will have hunters available to kite Dragons if we fall behind in DPS.

Phase 2 (Burn)

As soon as the last egg is destroyed, the Paladin who was controlling Razorgore will run to the tanks. The tanks will pick up Razorgore and stand at about a 90 degree angle from each other. Melee will be behind the boss. Razorgore will occasionally cast Conflagration on the tank so a secondary tank will be needed for this phase.

Vaelastrasz the Corrupt

Tanks: 2-5 Based on DPS

This fight is started by speaking with Vaelstrasz.

If the fight lasts longer than three minutes, it will most likely be a wipe. Tanks should find a good balance between threat and fire resist gear. 1.3 or 1.4 speed weapon suggested (or Eskhandar Claw)

Every 15 seconds, Vaelastrasz will cast Burning Adrenaline, which does increase damage by 100%, but will kill the target after 20 seconds. Anyone (besides your tank) who receives the buff needs to run far away from the rest of the raid to not kill anyone when they die. This should only be cast on mana users and the Main Tank every third time. When the tank gets the debuff, the next tank should start moving into position to take over.

The best way to position him is by having your warriors at least 90 degrees away from the current tank, near his left shoulder. Melee DPS should be on the other side of the boss, staying in between his front and rear right legs. All casters, ranged DPS, and healers should be next to the wall directly across from the warriors (melee DPS side) on the other side of Vaelestrasz.
